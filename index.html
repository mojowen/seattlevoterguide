About What’s Inside

This non-partisan voter guide was developed around the Seattle Youth Agenda, a set of issues prioritized through surveying youth across Seattle and in collaboration with other youth organizations. We asked candidates questions about the Youth Agenda Issues; police accountability, housing accessibility, climate justice and youth employment. The guide includes responses from city council candidates and information on ballot measures. This guide should not be viewed as an endorsement of any candidates or ballot measures. To get a more comprehensive voter guide, visit seattlevoterguide.org.


Brought to you by

The Washington Bus is a non-partisan, non-profit, youth-driven organization that increases political access and participation for young people across Washington State. The Bus engages young people on their own terms, and helps them build their own power through political education, civic and cultural engagement, and hands-on democracy. Ballot Measures

Initiative 122: Honest Elections Seattle

What it does

Would create a system to publically finance city campaigns

A yes Vote Means...

Creating a city wide program for public financing that gives $100 in vouchers to each registered voter to contribute to candidates of their choice. This measure also requires all paid signature gatherers to wear badges identifying they are paid.

endorse

A no Vote Means...

Business as usual. There is no effect on current campaign finance laws or elections.

endorse

Proposition 1: Let's Move Seattle

What it does

Replaces an expiring levy to fund transportation improvements & relieve congestion

A yes Vote Means...

Increased funding for Seattle transit systems, builds a network of seven bus rapid transit corridors, optimizes traffic signals on 45 heavily-traveled corridors, Safe Routes to Schools projects at all public schools.

endorse

A no Vote Means...

Exisiting tranist levy expires. Current funding goes away and new projects to alleviate traffic are not funded.

endorse
